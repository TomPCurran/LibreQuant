Design principles

Three pillars

Reactive research

Sculpt strategies, don’t only batch-run backtests. Parameters map to UI controls as you iterate.

Zero-trust locality

Strategies, data, and keys stay in your Docker environment—nothing shipped to a black-box cloud.

Workflow ownership

One surface from idea → backtest → live deployment → monitoring—without losing context.

AST Discovery & Parameter Bridge

Discover tunable parameters from your strategy source; wire them to sliders and controls without hand-built glue code.

Zero-Trust Local Execution

Stationarity checks and orders stay on your machine: local broker adapters, no cloud middleman, keys under your control.

cd LibreQuant

# Launch
docker compose up -d

FAQ

How is this different from Jupyter + scripts?
Jupyter is great for exploration; LibreQuant adds AST discovery so parameters and UI stay in sync with your strategy file—fewer ad-hoc widgets and copy-pasted constants.
Where does execution run?
In your local Docker environment by default. You keep custody of data and credentials; the workbench is the Assistant, not a hosted black box.
What about drawdowns and bad backtests?
Risk is yours to model. The UI can surface equity and stress metrics; strategy validity still depends on your assumptions, stationarity checks, and out-of-sample discipline—never treat a green curve as a guarantee.
